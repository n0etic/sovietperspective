About the project

This project investigates how Cold War events—such as the Korean War, Berlin Wall, and Cuban Missile Crisis—have been presented in Soviet and Russian textbooks from the 1960-80s, 1990s, and 2021. These textbooks reveal shifting narratives, from early Soviet portrayals of the USSR as a savior against American aggression, to more balanced accounts in the post-Soviet 1990s, and more recent revisions reflecting current political agendas.

The project offers insights into the use of propaganda by governments to shape historical memory and influence public perception. By studying how these narratives have changed, it highlights how educational content serves as a powerful tool for disseminating propaganda and shaping collective understanding of global events.
